New Free LLMs.txt Generator Tool Released Based on Extensive Analysis of 137,000 Domains

ahrefs.com · Fri, 24 Jul 2026 09:52:42 +0000

A new free tool based on an analysis of 137,000 domains helps webmasters and agencies create optimized llms.txt files for AI readability and generative engine optimization.

A new free online tool designed to automatically generate standardized llms.txt files has officially been made available to the public. Developed through a comprehensive empirical study that analyzed over 137,000 internet domains, this innovative generator aims to simplify how webmasters, developers, and digital strategists present site content to artificial intelligence systems. As AI models increasingly search, scrape, and process web data, maintaining a structured text file helps ensure that machine learning algorithms can accurately digest essential context about a website.

The creation of this specialized tool stems from key technical insights gathered during the extensive multi-domain study. By identifying recurring patterns, structural configurations, and common implementation errors across thousands of active websites, the generator constructs optimized files specifically tailored for AI readability. It directly addresses the rising demand from digital marketing specialists, optimization consultants, and technical teams who need to improve how their online presence is interpreted by modern generative AI platforms and discovery engines.

Industry professionals are encouraged to adopt and share this resource across their professional networks, including corporate executives, colleagues, clients, and generative engine optimization (GEO) agencies. By streamlining the creation of llms.txt files, the generator helps bridge the operational gap between traditional web development standards and next-generation search systems. As AI-driven content discovery continues to transform the digital ecosystem, this free utility provides an efficient method for businesses to keep their digital assets accessible, compliant, and effectively indexed.
