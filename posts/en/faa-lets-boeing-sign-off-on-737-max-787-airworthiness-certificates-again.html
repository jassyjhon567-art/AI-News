FAA Restores Boeing's Authority to Issue Airworthiness Certificates for 737 Max and 787 Jets

www.cnbc.com · Fri, 17 Jul 2026 19:38:06 GMT

The Federal Aviation Administration has restored Boeing's authority to issue airworthiness certificates for its 737 Max and 787 jets, signaling a major vote of confidence from the government.

The U.S. Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) has officially restored Boeing's authority to issue individual airworthiness certificates for its 737 Max and 787 Dreamliner aircraft. This significant regulatory shift allows the aerospace giant to conduct final safety inspections and sign off on newly manufactured planes before they are delivered to airline customers. This critical responsibility had been stripped from the company following a series of high-profile safety crises, software failures, and production halts that severely damaged its reputation.

For several years, the FAA maintained direct, hands-on oversight of every newly manufactured 737 Max and 787 jet, with federal inspectors personally examining each aircraft and issuing safety certificates. This strict protocol was implemented to restore public and industry trust after the catastrophic 737 Max crashes and subsequent manufacturing flaws discovered in the 787 assembly lines. The decision to return this delegation of authority to Boeing indicates that the federal regulator is now satisfied with the company's updated quality control measures and restructured safety protocols.

Industry analysts view this development as a major operational milestone and a crucial vote of confidence in Boeing from the United States government. While the FAA will continue to monitor Boeing’s assembly lines and production facilities closely, the restoration of self-certification is expected to significantly streamline the delivery process, helping the company clear its substantial backlog of aircraft and stabilize its financial standing. For its part, Boeing has pledged to maintain its rigorous safety standards to ensure ongoing compliance with federal regulations.
