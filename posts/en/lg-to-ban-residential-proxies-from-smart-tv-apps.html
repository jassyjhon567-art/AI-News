LG to Ban Smart TV Apps Operating as Residential Proxy Nodes

krebsonsecurity.com · Wed, 22 Jul 2026 01:10:38 +0000

LG Electronics USA is banning webOS smart TV apps that secretly act as residential proxies, following research showing over 42 percent of store apps routed unknown third-party traffic.

LG Electronics USA has officially announced plans to suspend and ban all applications on its smart TV platform that convert user televisions into always-on residential proxy nodes. The strategic decision is aimed at significantly enhancing consumer privacy and digital security across devices running the company's proprietary webOS operating system. By preventing third-party applications from quietly leveraging household internet connections to route external web traffic, LG seeks to prevent potential network abuse, protect consumer bandwidth, and maintain the integrity of its smart ecosystem.

The decisive policy update comes less than a month after cybersecurity researchers uncovered a widespread issue within LG's official application marketplace. Their detailed investigation revealed that more than 42 percent of games and utility applications available for download on the webOS store contained embedded residential proxy software. These hidden mechanisms allowed unknown third parties to route their own internet traffic through consumers' home IP addresses. Security analysts highlighted that such proxy networks are frequently utilized by unauthorized entities to carry out automated web scraping, credential stuffing attacks, and other covert cyber operations while concealing their true origin.

To address these serious vulnerability risks, LG is actively auditing its software library and requiring developers to eliminate proxy-related code immediately. Developers who fail to comply with the updated platform standards face prompt removal from the webOS app store. This aggressive enforcement highlights a broader effort within the consumer electronics sector to dismantle covert proxy networks embedded in connected household appliances, ensuring that smart TVs remain secure entertainment hubs rather than unauthorized gateways for global internet traffic.
