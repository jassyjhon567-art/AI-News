Mark Cuban Envisions Future Where AI Simulators Train Workers Like Pilots and Drivers

www.businessinsider.com · 2026-07-24T12:48:46Z

Tech investor Mark Cuban predicts artificial intelligence will revolutionize job training by using immersive simulations similar to those used by pilots and race car drivers.

Billionaire entrepreneur and tech investor Mark Cuban believes that artificial intelligence will fundamentally transform how future generations prepare for the workforce. Speaking on the evolution of modern technology, Cuban predicted that AI will enable job seekers and corporate employees to undergo highly immersive, simulation-based training programs. This futuristic approach would mirror the practical preparation traditionally reserved for high-stakes professions such as airline pilots, astronauts, and professional race car drivers, allowing workers to hone their skills in controlled environments.

According to Cuban, AI-driven digital environments will allow individuals across a wide array of industries to gain vital hands-on experience without facing real-world risks or incurring massive operational costs. Rather than relying entirely on traditional classroom lectures or static instruction manuals, trainees will navigate dynamic, real-time scenarios tailored to their specific occupational roles. These intelligent simulators are expected to continuously adapt to an individual's skill level, providing immediate personalized feedback, realistic problem-solving exercises, and repeatable scenarios to ensure true mastery before entering the field.

The widespread adoption of artificial intelligence simulations in workforce development could significantly bridge the growing divide between theoretical academic knowledge and practical job execution. As global industries become increasingly complex and technologically driven, such advanced training methods promise to accelerate employee onboarding, dramatically reduce costly workplace errors, and democratize access to high-skilled careers. Cuban’s optimistic forecast highlights a broader technological shift where interactive systems redefine vocational education, professional upskilling, and corporate training standards worldwide.
