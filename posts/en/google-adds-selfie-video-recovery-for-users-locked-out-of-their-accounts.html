Google Introduces Selfie Video Feature to Help Users Recover Locked Accounts

thehackernews.com · Thu, 23 Jul 2026 15:30:00 +0530

Google has introduced a new selfie video account recovery feature to help users regain access to locked accounts when traditional verification methods are unavailable.

Global technology giant Google has officially announced a new account recovery feature that enables users to regain access to their locked accounts by submitting a short selfie video. This innovative verification option serves as a supplemental tool alongside existing traditional recovery methods, such as receiving authentication codes through a backup email address or a registered mobile phone number. The new mechanism is specifically designed to assist individuals who have lost access to their primary security credentials or are completely unable to navigate standard sign-in procedures.

When users face sign-in issues and choose the video verification route, the system will prompt them to record a short, real-time video of their face using a mobile device or webcam. Google's automated authentication system will then evaluate the video footage to verify the user's identity against historical security parameters connected to the account. This streamlined process offers a highly crucial lifeline for users who no longer possess physical security keys, active phone lines, or secondary backup devices required during standard multi-factor authentication steps.

In response to potential privacy and data security considerations, Google emphasized that all selfie video recordings submitted for recovery purposes will be processed securely under strict safety protocols. The company stated that the video data will be used solely for identity verification and will be permanently deleted once the account review process is finalized. As online security threats continue to evolve, this new biometric option provides a robust fallback mechanism to help users protect and safely recover their valuable digital profiles.
