New York High School Adopts AI Robot Assistant, Sparking Pushback from Parents and Teachers

www.cnet.com · 2026-07-24T13:00:00Z

A New York high school's plan to deploy an AI-powered humanoid robot for classroom and homework support has faced strong backlash from parents and teachers union leaders.

A high school in New York is set to introduce an artificial intelligence-powered humanoid robot to its classrooms, sparking significant debate among educators and community members. Developed by tech firm Realbotix, the innovative system is designed to serve as an interactive assistant in school while offering AI-driven homework support to students at home. Supporters argue that integrating advanced robotics into the curriculum could help modernize instruction and better prepare students for an increasingly digital future.

However, the initiative has met strong resistance from both parents and school staff who harbor deep concerns over privacy, pedagogical impact, and ethical boundaries. The local teachers union strongly criticized the project, emphasizing that students should not be treated as "lab rats" in an experimental trial. Opponents are demanding greater transparency from administrators, calling for thorough evaluations and clear safety guidelines before allowing autonomous humanoid technology to interact directly with children in an educational setting.

As artificial intelligence becomes more prevalent in educational institutions nationwide, school districts are facing the complex task of balancing innovation with traditional learning standards. While the school administration views the robot assistant as a promising tool to augment teaching efforts, critical stakeholders remain skeptical about its long-term necessity and effectiveness. The ongoing controversy highlights broader societal anxieties regarding the rapid rise of AI and its expanding role in shaping youth development.
