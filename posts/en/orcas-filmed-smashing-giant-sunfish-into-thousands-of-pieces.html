Researchers Film Orcas Using Teamwork to Shatter Giant Sunfish in Unprecedented Discovery

www.sciencedaily.com · Thu, 23 Jul 2026 09:55:59 EDT

Researchers in the Gulf of California documented a novel orca behavior where one whale holds a giant sunfish while another rams it to shatter the carcass into bite-sized pieces.

Marine researchers in the Gulf of California have captured extraordinary footage documenting a dramatic and previously undescribed feeding behavior among killer whales. During two separate scientific encounters, observers witnessed a pod of orcas employing an unprecedented tactical strategy to dismantle giant sunfish, scientifically known as Mola mola. Rather than relying on conventional tearing or biting methods, the marine apex predators engaged in a highly coordinated, high-impact maneuver that completely pulverized the large fish, surprising wildlife experts worldwide.

According to field observations, the unique tactic required exceptional precision, timing, and teamwork between individual whales. One orca would firmly grip the dead sunfish by its prominent fin, holding the large carcass steady in the water column. Simultaneously, a second orca retreated to build momentum before charging directly into the fish at maximum speed. The resulting violent collision instantly blasted the carcass into thousands of smaller pieces, scattering manageable bits of food that the pod could readily consume.

Marine biologists suggest that this specialized ramming behavior underscores the advanced cognitive capabilities, social learning, and adaptability of distinct killer whale populations. Sunfish feature remarkably thick, tough skin that poses a challenge for marine predators attempting to tear them apart using standard dental force alone. By leveraging dynamic mechanical force and cooperative alignment, the orcas demonstrated an innovative feeding mechanism, providing researchers with invaluable new insights into the complex hunting strategies of marine mammals.
