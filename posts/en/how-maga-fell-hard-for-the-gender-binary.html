How the MAGA Movement Embraced Hyper-Masculinity and the Gender Binary

www.ft.com · Sun, 19 Jul 2026 04:00:20 GMT

The MAGA movement's promotion of hyper-masculinity and traditional gender roles, exemplified by Pete Hegseth, reflects a broader conservative effort to reshape American military and cultural policies.

The modern American right-wing movement, particularly the MAGA faction, has increasingly centered its cultural identity around rigid gender binaries and hyper-masculinity. This ideological focus is vividly illustrated by figures like Pete Hegseth, whose public advocacy for promoting testosterone levels among military troops has drawn significant attention. Hegseth’s positions reflect a broader aesthetic within the conservative movement that equates national strength with traditional, biological notions of manhood. This aesthetic has moved from the fringes of online subcultures to the mainstream of conservative political strategy, influencing policy proposals and leadership selections.

This emphasis on traditional masculinity is not merely rhetorical; it has direct implications for defense policy and military culture. Proponents of this view argue that the armed forces must prioritize physical combat readiness and discard what they view as distracting diversity, equity, and inclusion initiatives. By framing military effectiveness through the lens of high testosterone and traditional male roles, the movement seeks to dismantle recent progressive reforms aimed at making the military more inclusive for women and LGBTQ+ service members. Critics, however, warn that defining military capability so narrowly ignores the complex, technological nature of modern warfare, which requires diverse cognitive skills and technical expertise alongside physical endurance.

Ultimately, this obsession with the gender binary serves as a powerful unifying symbol for the conservative base. It positions physical strength and traditional gender roles as essential bulwarks against what conservatives perceive as societal and cultural decline. As this aesthetic continues to shape political discourse, it highlights a deep ideological divide in America over the definition of strength, leadership, and the future of national institutions. What began as a cultural talking point has now solidified into a core policy objective, signaling a determined effort to reshape American society along traditionalist lines.
