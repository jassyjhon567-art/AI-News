Peak Design Introduces Modular Field Bracket with Built-in Location Tag Integration

www.theverge.com · 2026-07-21T14:12:02-04:00

Peak Design’s new modular Field Bracket seamlessly integrates location-tracking tags into camera setups, offering photographers a sleek and secure way to prevent equipment loss.

Photographers and digital content creators frequently transport thousands of dollars worth of sensitive camera equipment, making gear loss or theft a constant, nerve-wracking anxiety during travel. In recent years, many professionals and hobbyists have turned to Bluetooth tracking devices, such as Apple AirTags, to keep tabs on their valuable property. However, physically securing these bulky tags to sleek camera bodies often results in awkward, dangling setups that can easily snag, detach, or interfere with comfortable handling during fast-paced photo shoots.

Addressing this practical dilemma, popular gear manufacturer Peak Design has introduced its latest innovation: a modular Field Bracket featuring built-in location tag integration. Designed to seamlessly incorporate a finder tag directly into the camera's mounting infrastructure, this accessory eliminates the need for unsightly straps, zip ties, or makeshift adhesive mounts. The low-profile housing securely encloses the tracker within a durable frame, while maintaining full compatibility with Peak Design’s widely used quick-release plates, tripods, and carrying systems.

By offering a discrete, reliable, and elegant solution for real-time equipment tracking, the modular Field Bracket resolves a major headache for adventurous travelers and prone-to-forgetfulness creators alike. Photographers can now confidently navigate busy airports, public transportation, and remote locations with enhanced peace of mind. Ultimately, this thoughtful design update demonstrates how functional utility and sleek security can coexist seamlessly within modern camera gear ecosystems, protecting high-value investments without compromising operational efficiency.
