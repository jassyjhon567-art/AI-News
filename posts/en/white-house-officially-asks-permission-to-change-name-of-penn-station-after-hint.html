White House Formally Seeks Approval to Rename New York's Penn Station

fortune.com · Fri, 24 Jul 2026 12:39:33 +0000

The White House has officially requested to rename New York's Pennsylvania Station after Donald Trump, following revealed design renderings that already feature his name and presidential seal inside the hub.

The White House has officially submitted a formal request to rename New York City's historic Pennsylvania Station, following weeks of persistent speculation regarding potential rebranding efforts for the major transit hub. Administration officials had previously hinted that the sprawling facility might be renamed in honor of Donald Trump, bringing national attention to the future identity of one of the busiest passenger transportation centers in North America. The official request initiates what is expected to be a contentious review process involving multiple governmental bodies and transit agencies overseeing the region's infrastructure.

The administrative step follows the recent public emergence of architectural renderings and preliminary design mock-ups that display the presidential seal alongside Trump's name prominently emblazoned across interior walls of the station. These visual concepts indicate that detailed spatial and aesthetic planning was underway long before any formal authorization had been granted by state or local authorities. The existence of these pre-made design renderings has sparked intense debate among urban planners, historic preservationists, and elected representatives concerning procedural transparency and public input in major infrastructure decisions.

Renaming a landmark transportation hub of this magnitude typically requires navigating a complex network of federal approvals, state legislation, and regional transit board consensus. Critics of the initiative argue that preserving the historic identity of Pennsylvania Station is paramount, while supporters view the proposal as a fitting recognition of presidential legacy within a major public space. As the request proceeds through official administrative channels, public hearings and legal assessments will ultimately determine whether the iconic station undergoes its first major name change in over a century.
